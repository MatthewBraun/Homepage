Course Description and Objectives

This course explores the first five phases of the Systems Development Life Cycle including scope definition, problem analysis, requirements analysis, logical design and decision analysis with the goal of determining an effective system solution. Topics covered include Use Case development, gap analysis, financial analysis of IT investments, and feasibility analysis. Prerequisites for this course are APC 300, APC 320 and APC 330.

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Describe the Systems Development Life Cycle (SDLC).
  • Create a project plan.
  • Understand feasibility studies and development strategies.
  • Create requirements
  • Construct process and data models.
  • Produce a system design satisfying requirements and constraints.
  • Describe system architecture.
  • Understand systems implementation processes.
  • Describe strategies for managing systems support and security.

Expectations

Communication

I expect if something is not clear you ask. In fact, I make no apologies if my assignments are not clear. When trying to understand what the customer wants, it is rarely clearly articulated. It is your job to clarify and ask questions. So here is a place to start practicing asking good questions and questions that help clarify the desire of the customer.

This term we will be using Piazza for Q&A. The system is designed to get you help fast and efficiently from classmates and myself. Rather than emailing questions, I encourage you to post your questions on Piazza.

On my part, when I am asked a question, I plan to share with the entire group the question and the answer. These will be shared in Piazza. You post anonymously in Piazza (or you can email me). I’ll only post the question then to the forum.

I do plan on adding something to the newsfeed weekly. Most likely that will happen over the weekend so be sure you check in early in the week.

Feedback

You can expect that every assignment and project part will have feedback attached. With each lesson there is at least one assignment/project part that is due. It is my intention to give you feedback weekly at a minimum.

Workload

Since this is an online course, it is expected that the student will spend approximately 40 hours of required reading, 56 hours of group project work, 46 hours completing assignments and 8 hours for exams (2 exams will be 2 hours and the final exam will be 4 hours).
