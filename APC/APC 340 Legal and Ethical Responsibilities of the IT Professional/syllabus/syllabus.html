Course Description and Objectives

This course explores a range of legal, regulatory, ethical and compliance issues associated with developing software and using information systems in an organization. Topics include the ethical and legal issues associated with data privacy and intellectual property, compliance with regulatory requirements such as Sarbanes Oxley, and other related contemporary subjects.

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  • Identify and explain the ethical issues of IT professionals.
  • Identify and explain the ethical issues of IT organizations.
  • Identify and explain the ethical responsibilities of IT professionals.
  • Identify and explain the ethical responsibilities of IT organizations.
  • Identify and explain the regulatory requirements of the IT organization.
  • Identify and explain the compliance requirements of the IT organization.

Expectations

Course Work

All written work should be formatted with one-inch margins on all sides and single-spaced using a 12-point font. Clarity, grammar and spelling will count in the evaluation of your written assignments and e-mail communications. It is expected that all communications will be prepared and presented in a professional manner.

Team Project

Over the course of the semester, you will work as part of a team to develop a code of conduct for a new and fictional IT firm. The instructor will place you on a team. All instructions for the project are posted on D2L. 

Online Discussions

Participation in online discussions is an important component of your grade. One of the best ways to appreciate the ethical and legal issues impacting our profession is to talk them out through thoughtful discussion and deliberation. Each week we will discuss (on D2L) questions/topics related to materials from the text or other readings. Collectively, we will tease out the ethical dilemmas and appropriate potential responses. One of the most important advantages of collaborative learning is the opportunity it provides to interact with others who, like you, are future leaders of industry. Do not deprive yourself of this important opportunity.

Written Assignments

During the semester you will complete some written assignments. These include position papers, case analyses, and a statement of your own professional code of ethics. Specific guidance for each of these can be located on D2L along with the rubrics I will use to evaluate your work. Please check the Course Calendar for due dates.

Final Exam

The final exam will be an oral exam conducted via Skype. I will work with you to schedule these in the final week of the semester. The exam will be comprised of questions drawn from the coursework and readings. I will assign each question a number. At the start of the exam, you will be asked to choose a set of numbers. I will then ask you the questions which correspond with your selections. I may also ask you follow-up questions based on your initial response to probe for depth of understanding. This allows me to help redirect you should you stray from the intended question. The exam should take approximately 30 minutes to complete.

Academic Misconduct

At all times, you are expected to abide by the student conduct policies of the University including those regarding academic honesty as outlined in Chapter UWS 14, Wisconsin Administrative Code. Section 14.01 of the code states the following: "Students are responsible for the honest completion and representation of their work, for the appropriate citation of sources, and for respect of others' academic endeavors." Any violation of this policy will be escalated for review and may be punishable by failure on an exam or assignment, failure of this course, or expulsion from the college. All work for this course is to be completed individually unless the work assigned is explicitly identified as collaborative or group work.
